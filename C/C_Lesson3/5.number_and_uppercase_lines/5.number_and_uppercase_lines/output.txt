1 | STEEL IS AN ALLOY OF IRON AND CARBON WITH IMPROVED STRENGTH AND FRACTURE RESISTANCE COMPARED TO OTHER FORMS OF IRON.
2 | BECAUSE OF ITS HIGH TENSILE STRENGTH AND LOW COST, STEEL IS ONE OF THE MOST COMMONLY MANUFACTURED MATERIALS
3 | IN THE WORLD. STEEL IS USED IN BUILDINGS, AS CONCRETE REINFORCING RODS, IN BRIDGES, INFRASTRUCTURE, TOOLS,
4 | SHIPS, TRAINS, CARS, BICYCLES, MACHINES, ELECTRICAL APPLIANCES, FURNITURE, AND WEAPONS.
5 | IRON IS ALWAYS THE MAIN ELEMENT IN STEEL, BUT MANY OTHER ELEMENTS MAY BE PRESENT OR ADDED. STAINLESS STEELS,
6 | WHICH ARE RESISTANT TO CORROSION AND OXIDATION, TYPICALLY ARE 11% CHROMIUM.
7 | IRON IS THE BASE METAL OF STEEL. DEPENDING ON THE TEMPERATURE, IT CAN TAKE TWO CRYSTALLINE FORMS (ALLOTROPIC FORMS):
8 | BODY-CENTRED CUBIC AND FACE-CENTRED CUBIC.
